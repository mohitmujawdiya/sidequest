# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People who feel stuck in a dull day and want a small, concrete real-world prompt that makes life feel more playful. They may be browsing casually on a phone, checking during a break, or looking for a low-friction nudge before going outside or messaging someone.

## Product Purpose

Sidequest helps users draw curated life quests, filter them by difficulty and mood, save promising ones, and mark completed ones. Success means the user can get from bored to a specific doable action in seconds, without relying on AI-generated filler.

## Brand Personality

Warm, playful, and quietly adventurous. The app should feel like a cozy quest board for normal life: a little gamey, a little mischievous, but still modern and trustworthy.

## Anti-references

Do not copy the retro arcade look of the reference site. Avoid pixel-art nostalgia, neon-on-black game UI, generic SaaS hero sections, AI-generated quest copy, heavy purple gradients, emojis as interface icons, and dense gamification that makes simple real-world actions feel like chores.

## Design Principles

- The quest is the product: make the current suggestion feel tangible, readable, and worth doing.
- Playfulness should reduce friction, not add rules for their own sake.
- Curated beats generated: every quest should feel authored, specific, and safe.
- Sign-in enhances the loop, but browsing should still work without an account.
- Use familiar controls and clear feedback so the app feels trustworthy despite the playful premise.

## UI Copy & Casing

Use sentence case for headings, buttons, nav-adjacent labels, empty states, and toasts. Keep acronyms like XP uppercase. Use compact Title Case for short ritual or badge-like phrases when they sit beside other strong UI labels, such as "Browse -> Accept -> Do -> Remember."

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-friendly controls, visible focus states, reduced-motion-safe transitions, and language that does not assume a specific location, budget, friend group, physical ability, or comfort level.
